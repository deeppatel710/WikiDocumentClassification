The word Mojo refers to a magical charm bag used in hoodoo, and in modern usage may also refer to sexual potency. The term was made popular as the source of Austin Powers' power.
In media and entertainment:

MOJO HD, an American high-definition television network formerly known as INHD.
Mojo Pin, a song by Jeff Buckley, first playing in the album Grace, 1994
Mojo, Homer Simpson's helper monkey in an episode of The Simpsons
Mojo, a character in the video game Chrono Cross
Mojo (Mobile Journalist), reporters who spend their time in the field and send their stories electronically
Mojo (comics), a fictional supervillain in various Marvel Comics publications
Mojo, a film based on the play by Jez Butterworth
Mojo (magazine), a British music magazine
Mojo, a play by Jez Butterworth
Mojo: Conjure Stories, an anthology edited by Nalo Hopkinson
Box Office Mojo, a website detailing motion picture box-office totals
Mojo!, a video game similar in gameplay to Marble Madness
Mojo Jojo, a fictional supervillain in The Powerpuff Girls
MoJo, abbreviation for Mother Jones (magazine)

In music:

Got My Mojo Working, a 1956 song written by Preston Foster and performed by a number of different artists
"Mojo", a song by Peeping Tom on the album Peeping Tom
Mojo, a radio station produced by Bauer Radio
Mojo Club in Hamburg
King Mojo Club in Sheffield
Mojo Concerts, a Dutch subsidiary of Clear Channel Communications
Mr. Mojo Risin', a nickname and anagram of singer Jim Morrison of The Doors
